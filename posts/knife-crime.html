London on track for highest rates of knife crime in almost a decade

London is on track for its worst year for knife crime in almost a decade, with over a quarter of knife crime in London taking place in just five boroughs.

Data from the Met Police shows there have been 12,668 knife related crimes between January 2019 and October 2019 compared with 12,197 for the same period last year.

Knife crime in Westminster, Haringey, Southwark, Hackney, and Enfield made up 28% of knife crime in London this year.

This figure follows reports of 12 hours of bloodshed in London last week that saw three fatal stabbings, with a 22-year-old male being killed in Hackney.

Last year had the highest rates of knife crime recorded since 2010 but 2019 is set to overtake that number, with the death toll for this year already surpassing the 2018 figure.

As well as this year looking to be the worst for knife crime, April 2019 saw the highest number of knife related crimes since April 2010.

This record may extend past a nine-year high, as public data prior to April 2010 is unavailable.

These stats come amid Cressida Dick, the head of Scotland Yard saying in March that knife crime is the worst she has seen and that there is “some link” between falling police numbers and increased violent crime.

Westminster, Haringey, Hackney and Southwark also top the list for the highest numbers of knife crime per 10,000 people.

Haringey council responded to this saying: “Overall violence with injury has started to improve in Haringey, although robbery levels – which are linked to the increase in total knife crime – continue to escalate, and are a contributing factor to violence.”

The council added: “Knife crime is clustered geographically in Haringey, with partnership work being targeted in key hotspots in the borough.”

Westminster council have also attributed knife crime to the rise in robbery offences, with the council’s own research showing that there has been a 60% increase in robbery offences last year and according to the council “robbery is a key driver of knife crime”.

Westminster frequently sees the worst rates for knife crime, with hotspots in the West End and Hyde Park.
